IVB ASSESSMENT: Milner & Kubota (2005)
"Why the Move to Free Trade? Democracy and Trade Policy
 in the Developing Countries"

1. PAPER OVERVIEW
-----------------
Research Question: Does democracy cause trade liberalization?
Method: OLS with country fixed effects, AR1 correction, PCSE
Data: Panel of ~179 developing countries, 1970-1999
  - Actual estimation samples vary: N=649-774 (tariff), N=829-982 (SW)

Key Variables:
  Y (Outcome)   = TARIFF (avg statutory tariff rate, continuous 0-102)
                   or SW OPEN (Sachs-Warner openness, binary 0/1)
  D (Treatment) = REGIME (Polity IV score, -10 to +10, lagged 1 period)
                   Also: DICTATOR, ACLP as alternatives

2. EXACT REGRESSION SPECIFICATION (from paper p. 29, eq. 1)
-----------------------------------------------------------
tradepolicy_{i,t} = beta_0 + beta_1*REGIME_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_2*SIGNED_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_3*OFFICE_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_4*GDPPC_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_5*LNPOP_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_6*ECRISIS_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_7*BPC_{i,t-1}
                    + beta_8*AVOPEN_{-i,t-1}
                    + u_i + epsilon_{i,t}

Additional controls added in some specifications:
  - USHEG (US hegemony), GATT, FIVEOP, FDI, AV TARIFF

Notes on estimation:
  - Country fixed effects (u_i)
  - Time trend (DATE) or decade fixed effects
  - AR1 correction for serial correlation
  - Panel corrected standard errors (PCSE, Beck & Katz 1995)
  - All RHS variables lagged one period

5. IVB ASSESSMENT: COLLIDER ANALYSIS
-------------------------------------
For IVB, we need controls Z where Y (tariffs) -> Z (collider pathway).
The treatment is D = democracy/REGIME.

IVB formula: IVB_z = -theta*_z x pi_z
  where theta*_z = effect of Y on Z (conditional on D and other controls)
        pi_z = effect of D on Z (conditional on Y and other controls)

CONTROL-BY-CONTROL ASSESSMENT:

A. GDP per capita (GDP PC) - lagged
   Type: Time-varying, continuous
   Confounder? YES - economic development plausibly affects both
     democracy (modernization theory) and trade policy
   Collider? MODERATE
     Y -> Z channel: Lower tariffs -> more trade -> economic growth ->
     higher GDP per capita. Standard trade theory predicts trade
     liberalization boosts growth. However, the lag structure (GDP PC
     at t-1 predicting tariff at t) weakens this. The contemporaneous
     effect of tariffs on GDP is the concern, but lagging helps.
   D -> Z channel: YES - democracy may promote growth (Acemoglu et al.)
     or modernization theory says growth promotes democracy
   Assessment: MODERATE COLLIDER / STRONG CONFOUNDER
   IVB direction: With Amelia subset, theta* > 0 (higher tariffs ->
     higher GDP?? -- this is the within-FE relationship in 9 Asian
     countries, not generalizable). In the full sample, sign unclear.

B. Log Population (LN POP) - lagged
   Type: Time-varying, continuous (slowly changing)
   Confounder? WEAK - population is largely predetermined; hard to
     argue tariffs affect population in 1-year lag
   Collider? WEAK/NO
     Y -> Z channel: Very hard to argue tariff rates affect total
     population. Population is driven by fertility, mortality, and
     migration. Tariffs could affect migration at the margin, but
     this effect is tiny relative to total population.
   D -> Z channel: WEAK - democracy might marginally affect population
     through health policy effects on mortality, but this is a stretch
     at 1-year lag
   Assessment: WEAK/NO COLLIDER, WEAK CONFOUNDER
   IVB concern: MINIMAL

C. IMF SIGNED - lagged
   Type: Time-varying, binary (dummy)
   Confounder? YES - IMF conditionality may require both political
     reforms and trade liberalization
   Collider? MODERATE
     Y -> Z channel: Countries with high tariffs may be more likely to
     face economic problems requiring IMF bailouts. BUT the lag goes
     the wrong way here (SIGNED at t-1 -> tariff at t). The question
     is whether contemporaneous tariffs cause IMF agreements --
     high tariffs -> poor economic performance -> IMF agreement.
     This is plausible but indirect.
   D -> Z channel: Democracies may be more/less likely to sign IMF
     agreements (Vreeland 2003 argues democracies are more likely
     to use IMF as a scapegoat)
   Assessment: MODERATE COLLIDER (primarily as confounder)
   Note: Binary variable - IVB formula still applies but interpretation
   differs. Being binary limits the collider bias magnitude.

D. Economic Crisis (ECRISIS) - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, binary
   Confounder? YES - crisis may trigger both political change and
     trade reform
   Collider? MODERATE
     Y -> Z channel: High tariffs -> economic distortions -> crisis.
     Protectionism can contribute to economic imbalances that
     eventually trigger crises.
   D -> Z channel: Democracy -> economic management quality -> crisis
   Assessment: MODERATE COLLIDER / MODERATE CONFOUNDER

E. Balance of Payments Crisis (BPC) - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, binary (reserves < 3 months imports)
   Confounder? YES - BPC may force trade reform
   Collider? STRONG
     Y -> Z channel: High tariffs -> reduced imports initially but
     also reduced exports (retaliation, inefficiency) -> BOP problems.
     Also: tariff liberalization -> surge in imports -> BOP pressure.
     BOTH high and low tariffs can cause BPC through different
     channels, making the sign ambiguous.
   D -> Z channel: Democracy -> economic policy quality -> BPC
   Assessment: MODERATE COLLIDER (ambiguous sign)
   Note: In paper, BPC is defined as reserves < 3 months imports.
   The Amelia "intresmi" variable (international reserves/months
   of imports) is the continuous version of this.

F. OFFICE (years in office) - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, discrete (count)
   Confounder? POSSIBLE - new governments may both democratize and
     liberalize trade
   Collider? WEAK
     Y -> Z channel: Hard to argue tariff rates cause government
     tenure. Governments don't fall because of tariff levels per se.
   D -> Z channel: Democracy -> shorter average tenure (more
     regular turnover)
   Assessment: WEAK COLLIDER, POSSIBLE CONFOUNDER

G. GATT membership - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, binary (rarely changes)
   Confounder? YES - GATT membership may promote both democracy
     (through international engagement) and free trade
   Collider? WEAK
     Y -> Z channel: Countries with low tariffs are not necessarily
     more likely to join GATT (many joined with high tariffs).
     Paper actually finds GATT members have HIGHER tariffs.
   D -> Z channel: Democracies more likely to join international
     organizations
   Assessment: WEAK COLLIDER, MODERATE CONFOUNDER

H. FDI (foreign direct investment/GNP) - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, continuous
   Confounder? YES - FDI may lobby for trade barriers (tariff-jumping)
     or against them, and FDI may flow to democracies
   Collider? MODERATE-STRONG
     Y -> Z channel: STRONG - Tariff rates directly affect FDI
     decisions. High tariffs may attract tariff-jumping FDI
     (invest in country to avoid trade barriers), or low tariffs
     may attract efficiency-seeking FDI. Paper finds FDI positively
     related to tariffs (suggesting tariff-jumping motive).
   D -> Z channel: Democracies may attract more FDI (rule of law,
     property rights) or less (policy uncertainty)
   Assessment: MODERATE-STRONG COLLIDER
   IVB direction: theta* > 0 (higher tariffs -> more FDI), paper
   shows FDI coefficient positive. If pi > 0 (democracy -> more FDI),
   then IVB = -theta*pi < 0, biasing democracy coefficient toward
   zero (attenuating the negative effect).

I. AV TARIFF / AV OPEN (world average) - lagged [not in Amelia]
   Type: Time-varying, continuous, but NO within-country variation
     (same value for all countries in a given year)
   Confounder? YES (global trend affects all countries)
   Collider? NO
     Y -> Z channel: An individual country's tariff cannot cause
     the world average to change (except marginally for large countries).
   Assessment: NO COLLIDER - this is essentially a time trend control
   Note: Absorbed by time fixed effects / time trend

J. US Hegemony (USHEG)
   Type: Time-varying, but NO within-country variation
     (same value for all countries in a given year)
   Confounder? YES (US power may affect both democratization and
     trade liberalization globally)
   Collider? NO
     Y -> Z channel: Individual country tariffs cannot cause changes
     in US share of world trade.
   Assessment: NO COLLIDER - this is essentially a time trend
   Note: With only between-year variation, this is similar to a year
   fixed effect. In the Amelia dataset with 9 countries, usheg has
   almost no within-country variation.

K. FIVEOP (Quinn financial openness of 5 top economies)
   Type: Time-varying, but NO within-country variation
     (same value for all countries in a given year)
   Confounder? YES (global financial openness trend may affect both
     democratization pressure and trade liberalization)
   Collider? NO
     Y -> Z channel: Individual LDC tariffs cannot cause the top 5
     industrial countries to open their capital markets.
   Assessment: NO COLLIDER - this is a global/time trend variable

6. SUMMARY OF IVB POTENTIAL
---------------------------

STRONGEST COLLIDER CANDIDATES (worth computing IVB):
  1. FDI (lagged) - MODERATE-STRONG: tariffs directly affect FDI flows
  2. GDP per capita (lagged) - MODERATE: tariffs affect growth via trade
  3. BPC / international reserves - MODERATE: tariffs affect BOP

MODERATE COLLIDER CANDIDATES:
  4. IMF SIGNED - MODERATE: tariff-induced economic problems -> IMF
  5. ECRISIS - MODERATE: protectionism contributes to economic crises

WEAK/NO COLLIDER:
  6. LN POP - WEAK: tariffs don't meaningfully affect population
  7. OFFICE - WEAK: tariffs don't cause government tenure changes
  8. GATT - WEAK: tariff levels don't clearly cause GATT membership
  9. AV TARIFF, AV OPEN - NO: individual tariffs can't cause world avg
  10. USHEG, FIVEOP - NO: individual tariffs can't cause US hegemony/
      global financial openness

7. CAN WE COMPUTE IVB?
-----------------------

WITH AMELIA DATA (9 Asian countries):
  - PARTIAL. We can compute IVB for: GDP PC, LN POP, SIGNED, FIVEOP,
    USHEG (5 of the ~10 controls)
  - MISSING: ECRISIS, BPC (binary), OFFICE, GATT, FDI, AV TARIFF
  - The strongest collider candidate (FDI) is MISSING
  - Sample is too small (9 countries, ~100 usable obs) and not
    representative of the full paper's sample (~100 LDCs)
  - Results would NOT be meaningful as an IVB replication

WITH FULL REPLICATION DATA (from Milner's website):
  - COMPLETE. All variables should be available.
  - Need to manually download from Princeton page (requires browser)
  - The full dataset has 907 obs for tariffs, 100+ countries
  - Could compute IVB for all controls in Table 3

ASSESSMENT: This paper is a GOOD CANDIDATE for IVB analysis in
principle, but requires the FULL replication dataset, which must
be manually downloaded from Milner's Princeton page.

Key strengths as IVB candidate:
  + Clear OLS specification with country FE
  + Multiple continuous controls that are plausible colliders
  + FDI is an especially strong collider candidate
  + GDP per capita is a classic collider in political economy
  + Paper is highly cited (~1200+ citations)
  + Clean panel structure

Key limitations:
  - Amelia subset is insufficient (9 countries, missing key vars)
  - Need manual download of replication data
  - AR1 correction complicates exact replication
  - PCSE complicates standard error computation
  - Many controls are binary (ECRISIS, BPC, SIGNED, GATT)
    which limits IVB magnitude
  - Several controls are really time trends (USHEG, FIVEOP, AV TARIFF)
    with no within-country variation -> not colliders
