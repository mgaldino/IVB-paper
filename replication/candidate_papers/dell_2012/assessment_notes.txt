IVB Assessment: Dell, Jones & Olken (2012, AEJ:Macro)
"Temperature Shocks and Economic Growth: Evidence from the Last Half Century"
=============================================================================

VERDICT: BAD candidate for IVB analysis
REASON: No continuous time-varying controls beyond the treatment variables themselves.

=============================================================================
DETAILED ANALYSIS
=============================================================================

1. PAPER OVERVIEW
-----------------
- Uses country-year panel data (1950-2003, ~125 countries)
- Treatment (D): Temperature shocks (annual average temperature, degrees Celsius)
- Outcome (Y): GDP per-capita growth rate
- Also examines precipitation as a second treatment variable
- Identification: within-country year-to-year fluctuations in weather

2. MAIN SPECIFICATION (Equation 4, p.9 of manuscript)
------------------------------------------------------
g_it = theta_i + theta_rt + sum_{j=0}^{L} rho_j * T_{it-j} + epsilon_it

Where:
- g_it = per-capita GDP growth rate in country i, year t
- theta_i = country fixed effects
- theta_rt = time fixed effects (region x year FE and poor x year FE)
- T_it = vector of temperature and precipitation (and their interactions with poor dummy)
- epsilon_it = error term clustered at parent-country and year-region levels

3. WHAT ARE THE CONTROLS?
-------------------------
The ONLY right-hand-side variables in the main specification are:

A) Fixed effects (absorbed):
   - Country FE (theta_i)
   - Region x Year FE (theta_rt)
   - Poor country x Year FE

B) Time-varying regressors:
   - Temperature (and its lags)
   - Temperature x Poor dummy (and its lags)
   - Precipitation (and its lags)
   - Precipitation x Poor dummy (and its lags)

C) Time-invariant interaction terms:
   - "Poor" dummy (defined at first year of data entry): BINARY, NOT CONTINUOUS
   - "Hot" dummy (above median temperature in 1950s): BINARY, NOT CONTINUOUS
   - "Agricultural" dummy (above median ag share in 1995): BINARY, NOT CONTINUOUS

CRITICAL POINT: There are NO continuous time-varying controls beyond temperature
and precipitation themselves. The "poor", "hot", and "agricultural" dummies are
TIME-INVARIANT characteristics (defined at a fixed point in time) that are only
used as interaction terms with the treatment variable. They are fully absorbed
by country fixed effects.

4. CHANNEL/MECHANISM REGRESSIONS (Tables 5 and 6)
--------------------------------------------------
The paper examines channels in SEPARATE regressions where the dependent variable
changes (NOT as controls in the main growth regression):

Table 5: Growth in agriculture, industry, investment as DEPENDENT VARIABLES
Table 6: Political instability (POLITY, leader transitions, conflict) as DEPENDENT VARIABLES

These are separate reduced-form regressions to understand channels. None of these
variables ever appear as RIGHT-HAND-SIDE CONTROLS in the main GDP growth regression.

5. IVB REQUIREMENTS CHECK
--------------------------
Requirement 1: Linear OLS with FE? YES - panel OLS with country + region-year FE
Requirement 2: Continuous time-varying controls? NO - FAILS
   - Temperature and precipitation are the TREATMENT, not controls
   - The interaction dummies (poor, hot, agricultural) are time-invariant, binary,
     and absorbed by country FE
   - No additional time-varying controls are included
Requirement 3: Selection-on-observables design? PARTIALLY
   - Temperature is plausibly exogenous (weather shocks), so this is more of a
     natural experiment than selection-on-observables
   - The paper argues identification from year-to-year weather fluctuations
Requirement 4: Plausible colliders? NOT APPLICABLE
   - Since there are no time-varying controls, there is nothing to serve as a collider

6. WHY THIS PAPER IS A BAD CANDIDATE
--------------------------------------
The IVB formula requires IVB = -theta* x pi, where:
- theta* is the coefficient from regressing Y on Z (control) after partialing out D and FE
- pi is the coefficient from regressing D on Z after partialing out FE

For IVB to be computable, there must be at least one continuous time-varying control Z
that could plausibly be a collider (caused by both D and Y). This paper has NO such
controls. The specification is intentionally parsimonious: just weather variables and
fixed effects.

Even though the paper discusses channels (agriculture, industry, political stability)
that would be IDEAL collider candidates if they were included as controls, they are
NOT included in the main specification. They are only examined in separate regressions
as dependent variables.

7. COULD THE PAPER BE MODIFIED FOR IVB?
-----------------------------------------
In principle, if someone were to run the Dell et al. specification but ADD a control
like "political stability" or "agricultural output growth" to the right-hand side,
that would create an IVB-eligible specification. But the paper as published does not
do this, so there is no existing coefficient to analyze.
