STEP 2: PAPER ASSESSMENT FOR IVB ANALYSIS
=========================================================================

----- 2.1 IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY -----

The paper uses THREE identification strategies:
  (A) Dynamic linear panel model (within estimator / FE-OLS) -- Tables 2-4
  (B) Semi-parametric treatment effects (propensity score) -- Table 5
  (C) Instrumental variables (regional democracy waves) -- Table 6

The BASELINE specification (Equation 1) is:

  y_ct = beta * D_ct + sum_{j=1}^{p} gamma_j * y_{ct-j} + alpha_c + delta_t + epsilon_ct

where:
  - y_ct = log GDP per capita (country c, year t)
  - D_ct = dichotomous democracy measure (0/1)
  - y_{ct-j} = lags of log GDP per capita (typically 4 lags)
  - alpha_c = country fixed effects
  - delta_t = year fixed effects

CRITICAL: The BASELINE specification (Table 2) has NO time-varying
controls beyond lags of GDP itself. The only regressors are:
  - Democracy (binary treatment)
  - Lags of log GDP per capita
  - Country FE + Year FE

----- 2.2 DOES THE PAPER HAVE OLS/FE SPECIFICATIONS? -----

YES. Table 2, Columns 1-4 use the "within estimator" (i.e., OLS with
country and year fixed effects):
  - Col 1: 1 lag of GDP (N=6,790, 175 countries)
  - Col 2: 2 lags of GDP (N=6,642, 175 countries)
  - Col 3: 4 lags of GDP -- PREFERRED specification (N=6,336, 175 countries)
      Democracy coefficient: 0.787 (se=0.226)
      Long-run effect: 21.24% (se=7.21%)
  - Col 4: 8 lags of GDP (N=5,688, 175 countries)

Columns 5-8: Arellano-Bond GMM (not OLS -- excluded)
Columns 9-12: HHK estimator (not OLS -- excluded)

Table 3: Same as Table 2 but with GDP GROWTH RATE as dependent variable.
  Within estimator in Cols 1-4 is also OLS/FE.

Table 4: ROBUSTNESS with additional covariates (within estimator = OLS/FE):
  - Col 1: Baseline (same as Table 2, Col 3)
  - Col 2: GDP in 1960 quintiles x year effects
  - Col 3: Soviet dummies
  - Col 4: Lags of unrest (4 lags)
  - Col 5: Lags of trade (4 lags of import+exports/GDP)
  - Col 6: Lags of financial flows
  - Col 7: Lags of demographic structure (log pop, share <16, share >64)
  - Col 8: Region x regime x year effects

----- 2.3 WHAT ARE THE CONTROL VARIABLES? -----

In the BASELINE (Table 2, Col 3):
  - 4 lags of log GDP per capita (continuous, time-varying)
  - Country FE (absorbed)
  - Year FE (absorbed)

In the ROBUSTNESS (Table 4, Panel A within estimator):
  Col 4 adds: 4 lags of social unrest dummy
  Col 5 adds: 4 lags of trade share (imports+exports)/GDP
  Col 6 adds: 4 lags of net foreign assets/GDP
  Col 7 adds: 4 lags of log population + share pop <16 + share pop >64

----- 2.4 COLLIDER ASSESSMENT FOR IVB -----

For IVB, we need controls Z where:
  - D (democracy) --> Z  (treatment causes the control)
  - Y (GDP growth) --> Z  (outcome causes the control)
  - Possibly also Z --> Y and Z --> D (confounder role)

ASSESSMENT OF EACH CONTROL:

(a) LAGS OF LOG GDP PER CAPITA (y_{ct-1}, ..., y_{ct-4}):
    - These are LAGGED values of the outcome variable
    - D --> Z: Democracy at t could affect GDP at t+1, so past democracy
      affects current GDP lags. But D_ct does not cause y_{ct-1} through
      y_{ct-4} because those are PRIOR to treatment time.
    - Y --> Z: GDP at t does not cause GDP at t-1 through t-4 (temporal
      ordering prevents this).
    - VERDICT: NOT COLLIDERS. These are predetermined/lagged variables.
      They are confounders (past GDP affects both future democracy and
      future GDP), not colliders. Standard conditioning variable.

    HOWEVER, there is a subtlety: in a DYNAMIC panel, the lags of the
    dependent variable create a complex relationship. The Nickell bias
    is well-known. But this is not the same as IVB/collider bias.

(b) LAGS OF UNREST (Table 4, Col 4):
    - D --> Z: Democracy could affect unrest (democracy may reduce or
      increase social unrest). Table 7 shows democracy reduces unrest
      (coef = -7.832, within).
    - Y --> Z: GDP growth could affect unrest (economic crises trigger
      protests). Plausible reverse causation.
    - Z --> D: Unrest can trigger democratization (Arab Spring).
    - Z --> Y: Unrest reduces GDP growth.
    - VERDICT: POSSIBLE COLLIDER + CONFOUNDER (dual role). However,
      these are LAGGED unrest values (t-1 to t-4), not contemporaneous.
      Lagged unrest is plausibly predetermined relative to current D and
      current Y, reducing collider concerns.

(c) LAGS OF TRADE (Table 4, Col 5):
    - D --> Z: Democracy could increase trade openness. Table 7 shows
      positive but imprecise effect (coef=0.689, se=1.409).
    - Y --> Z: GDP growth increases trade (mechanical: denominator
      effect, and richer countries trade more).
    - Z --> D: Trade openness could affect democratization (exposure to
      democratic norms, economic interests).
    - Z --> Y: Trade affects GDP growth.
    - VERDICT: POSSIBLE COLLIDER + CONFOUNDER. But again these are
      LAGGED values. The authors themselves note: "these specifications
      need to be interpreted with some caution since trade and financial
      flows are endogenous to democracy."

(d) LAGS OF FINANCIAL FLOWS (Table 4, Col 6):
    - Similar logic to trade. Dual collider + confounder role possible.
    - But LAGGED values reduce concern.

(e) LAGS OF DEMOGRAPHIC STRUCTURE (Table 4, Col 7):
    - D --> Z: Democracy could affect population growth (through health
      policy, fertility policy). Slow-moving variable.
    - Y --> Z: GDP affects demographics (demographic transition).
    - VERDICT: Very slow-moving, largely predetermined. WEAK COLLIDER
      concern at best.

----- 2.5 KEY PROBLEM: BASELINE HAS NO TIME-VARYING CONTROLS -----

*** THIS IS THE CRITICAL ISSUE FOR IVB ***

The BASELINE specification (Table 2) -- which is the paper's MAIN
result -- includes ONLY:
  1. Democracy (binary treatment)
  2. Lags of log GDP per capita
  3. Country FE + Year FE

There are NO continuous time-varying controls that could serve as
colliders in the baseline specification. The lags of GDP are part of the
dynamic structure of the model, not "included variables" in the IVB sense.

The ROBUSTNESS specifications (Table 4) DO add time-varying controls,
but:
  - These are all LAGGED values (t-1 through t-4), not contemporaneous
  - They are included specifically as robustness checks
  - The paper shows the baseline results are NOT sensitive to including
    them (the democracy coefficient barely changes)
  - The authors treat these as potential confounders, not mechanisms

----- 2.6 WHAT ABOUT TABLE 7 (MECHANISMS)? -----

Table 7 estimates equation (7): m_ct = beta*D_ct + gamma*y_{ct-j} +
eta*m_{ct-j} + alpha_c + delta_t + epsilon_ct

This regresses MECHANISM VARIABLES on democracy:
  Col 1: Log investment share --> Democracy coef = 2.391 (se=1.114)
  Col 2: Log TFP --> Democracy coef = -0.205 (se=0.276)
  Col 3: Economic reforms index --> Democracy coef = 0.687 (se=0.348)
  Col 4: Log trade share --> Democracy coef = 0.689 (se=1.409)
  Col 5: Log tax share --> Democracy coef = 3.311 (se=0.338)
  Col 6: Log primary enrollment --> Democracy coef = 1.042 (se=0.338)
  Col 7: Log secondary enrollment --> Democracy coef = 1.345 (se=0.610)
  Col 8: Log child mortality --> Democracy coef = -0.253 (se=0.063)
  Col 9: Unrest dummy --> Democracy coef = -7.832 (se=2.185)

These are CHANNEL regressions (D --> Z, not Y ~ D + Z), so they could
potentially provide the pi values for IVB computation if we had a
specification that included these as controls in a GDP regression.

HOWEVER, there is no specification in the paper that simultaneously:
  (a) regresses GDP on democracy AND
  (b) includes these mechanism variables as controls

The authors deliberately AVOID controlling for mechanisms in the GDP
equation because they recognize these are channels/mediators, not
confounders.

STEP 3: VERDICT
=========================================================================

*** VERDICT: BAD CANDIDATE FOR IVB ANALYSIS ***

REASONS:

1. NO TIME-VARYING CONTROLS IN BASELINE SPECIFICATION
   The main specification (Table 2, Col 3) controls only for lags of GDP,
   country FE, and year FE. There are no "included variables" Z that could
   be colliders. The IVB formula IVB = -theta* x pi requires a control
   variable Z in the regression Y ~ D + Z + W, and no such Z exists in
   the baseline.

2. ROBUSTNESS CONTROLS ARE ALL LAGGED
   Table 4 adds lagged values of unrest, trade, financial flows, and
   demographics. Because these are lagged (t-1 to t-4), they are
   plausibly predetermined and less susceptible to collider bias. The
   temporal ordering weakens the Y --> Z_lag channel that is essential
   for collider bias.

3. TREATMENT IS BINARY (0/1)
   The democracy variable D_ct is dichotomous, not continuous. While FWL
   still applies algebraically, the interpretation of pi (coefficient of D
   in regression Z ~ D + W) with a binary treatment is a mean difference,
   which may not fit the IVB framework as naturally.

4. MAIN ESTIMATORS INCLUDE GMM/IV
   The paper's preferred specifications involve GMM (Arellano-Bond) and
   HHK estimators in addition to within-OLS. The IV specifications in
   Table 6 use regional democracy waves as instruments. IVB analysis
   requires pure OLS/FE specifications.

5. NO SPECIFICATION WITH MECHANISMS AS CONTROLS
   The paper intentionally does NOT include mechanism variables
   (investment, trade, schooling, etc.) as controls in the GDP regression.
   Table 7 only shows D --> mechanism regressions. Without a "long
   regression" that includes both D and mechanisms as regressors of GDP,
   we cannot compute theta*.

WHAT WOULD MAKE IT A GOOD CANDIDATE:
   If the paper had a specification like:
     y_ct = beta*D_ct + theta*investment_ct + theta*trade_ct +
            theta*schooling_ct + gamma*y_{ct-j} + alpha_c + delta_t + e_ct

   Then investment, trade, and schooling would be plausible colliders
   (caused by both D and Y), and we could compute IVB. But this
   specification does NOT exist in the paper.

  - But trade is LAGGED, weakening collider channel

=========================================================================
FINAL ASSESSMENT: BAD
=========================================================================
This paper is a BAD candidate for IVB analysis because:
(a) The baseline specification has no time-varying controls beyond GDP lags
(b) All robustness controls are lagged, reducing collider concerns
(c) Mechanism variables are analyzed separately, never included as
    controls in the GDP equation
(d) The treatment is binary
(e) The paper's design is explicitly dynamic panel, not
    selection-on-observables with a rich set of controls
